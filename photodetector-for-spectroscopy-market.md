<p><p>Photodetectors for spectroscopy are categorized based on the wavelength range they detect. The Ultraviolet Band targets shorter wavelengths (10-400 nm) for applications like chemical analysis. The Near Infrared Band (700-1400 nm) is crucial for applications in agricultural and biomedical fields. The Short-Wavelength Infrared Band (1400-3000 nm) and Mid-Wavelength Infrared Band (3000-8000 nm) are vital for material characterization and thermal imaging, while "Other" encompasses specialized detectors for unique applications.</p></p>
