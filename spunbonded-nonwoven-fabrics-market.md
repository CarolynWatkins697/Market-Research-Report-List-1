<p><p>Spunbonded nonwoven fabrics are classified primarily into three types based on polymer composition: polypropylene (PP), polyethylene (PE), and polyethylene terephthalate (PET). PP is widely used for its lightweight, strong, and cost-effective properties, making it suitable for applications like medical textiles and packaging. PE offers excellent moisture resistance and flexibility, commonly used in agricultural and hygiene products. PET, known for its durability and strength, is often employed in automotive and filtration sectors. Each type serves unique market needs driven by specific applications.</p></p>
