<p><p>The atherosclerosis therapeutics market comprises two primary types: small molecules and biologics. Small molecules include statins, PCSK9 inhibitors, and antihypertensives, which are chemically synthesized and administered to regulate cholesterol levels and improve vascular health. Biologics, on the other hand, consist of larger, complex molecules like monoclonal antibodies, targeting specific pathways involved in atherosclerosis progression. Both categories aim to reduce cardiovascular risks, but their mechanisms, delivery methods, and development processes differ, catering to diverse patient needs in managing this chronic condition.</p></p>
