<p><p>High Intensity Discharge (HID) lighting refers to a type of electric light that produces illumination by creating an arc between two electrodes in a gas-filled tube, which emits a bright, intense light. Common types of HID lights include metal halide, high-pressure sodium, and mercury vapor lamps. These lights are widely used in outdoor lighting applications such as streetlights, stadiums, and parking lots, as well as in industrial and commercial settings due to their high luminous efficacy and long service life.</p><p>The HID lighting market is witnessing significant growth driven by the increasing demand for energy-efficient lighting solutions and advancements in lighting technology.
