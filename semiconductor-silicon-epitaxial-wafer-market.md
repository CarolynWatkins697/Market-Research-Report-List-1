<p><p>The semiconductor silicon epitaxial wafer market is divided into P-type and N-type categories based on the doping element. P-type wafers are doped with elements like boron, creating a positive charge carrier, while N-type wafers use elements like phosphorus, resulting in negatively charged carriers. This differentiation is crucial for various semiconductor applications, as each type offers distinct electrical properties suited for devices like transistors and diodes. The demand for these wafers is driven by advancements in electronics and renewable energy technologies.</p></p>
