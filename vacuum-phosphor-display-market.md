<p><p>The Vacuum Phosphor Display (VPD) market is categorized into two main types: Statically Driven Displays and Dynamically Driven Displays. Statically Driven Displays utilize fixed patterns or images, providing consistent visual output ideal for applications needing steady information, like instrumentation. Conversely, Dynamically Driven Displays are designed to update images in real-time, making them suitable for dynamic data presentation, such as graphic displays and video content. Both types leverage the unique properties of phosphor materials under vacuum conditions to produce high-quality visuals.</p></p>
