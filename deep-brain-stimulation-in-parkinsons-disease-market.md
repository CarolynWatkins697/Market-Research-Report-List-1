<p><p>Deep Brain Stimulation (DBS) is an advanced therapeutic procedure used to alleviate symptoms in individuals with Parkinson's Disease. This technique can significantly improve motor function, reduce tremors, and enhance the quality of life for patients.</p><p>The market for DBS in Parkinson's Disease is experiencing notable growth, driven by an increasing prevalence of Parkinson's and advancements in technology. The integration of more sophisticated neurostimulation devices and improved surgical techniques enhances treatment effectiveness, attracting greater demand. Additionally, the rising aging population globally fuels the market, as Parkinson's is more common in older adults.</p><p>Innovative trends such as closed-loop systems, which adjust stimulation based on feedback from brain activity, and battery technology improvements are shaping the future of DBS.
